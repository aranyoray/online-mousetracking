Purpose:
Hello. We are conducting a research study to examine how judge the ability of agents. You must be 18 years of age or older to participate in this study.

Brief description:
In this study, you will complete a short categorization task. Then you will reflect on how you feel and answer some questions. The estimated time to complete this study is approximately 3 minutes and you’ll receive $.84 in exchange for participating.

Procedures:
The procedure.

Risks and Benefits:
There are no known or anticipated risks to you for participating. Although this study will not benefit you personally, we hope that our results will add to the knowledge about social perception and learning.

Confidentiality:
All of your responses will be anonymous. Only the researchers involved in this study and those responsible for research oversight will have access to the information you provide in our Qualtrics survey system and password protected server. Your electronic responses will be numbered and stored on a secure server. Your Prolific ID will be used in order to pay you for your time, but we have no access to any public profiles or identifiable information in connection with your ID. You will be paid through Prolific as usual using the submission code and approval system.

Voluntary Participation:
Participation in this study is completely voluntary. You are free to decline to participate, to end participation at any time for any reason, or to refuse to answer any individual question without penalty. Your decision whether or not to participate in this study will not affect your relationship with our lab on Prolific. We cannot inform you of the entire nature of the study at this time, but we will explain the full purpose at the end of the survey. If you have any questions about this study, you may contact the investigator, Nicholas Surdel, at Nicholas.surdel@yale.edu In line with Prolific’s community guidelines, you are liable to forfeiting compensation (i.e., rejected submission) if you meet any of the following criteria: (1) Incomplete study data, or submitting ‘nocode’, (2) failing one or more attention checks, (3) giving intentionally low-effort responses, or (4) being identified as using a VPN or non-human response interface.
